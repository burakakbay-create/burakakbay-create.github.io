Privacy Policy — Lazy Dad

Last updated: 13 July 2026 · Published by BIGCLOUDGAMES

This Privacy Policy explains how the mobile game Lazy Dad (“the App”, “we”, “us”) handles information. Lazy Dad is a single-player, offline comedy game published by BIGCLOUDGAMES. We designed it to collect as little data as possible and to not track you.

Summary (plain language)
  • We do not require an account and do not ask for your name, email, or contacts.
  • We do not track you across apps or websites, and we do not use Apple’s advertising identifier (IDFA) for tracking. The App shows only non-personalized ads.
  • Your game progress is stored only on your device (encrypted).
  • The only data leaving your device is what is needed to (a) show non-personalized ads, (b) process an optional in-app purchase, and (c) submit an optional leaderboard score.

Information we handle

1. On-device game data (not collected by us). Your level progress, settings, language choice and “Remove Ads” ownership are saved locally on your device (via encrypted local storage). This never leaves your device except through the platform’s own iCloud/backup mechanisms, which you control.

2. Advertising (Google AdMob) — non-personalized only. The App displays interstitial and rewarded ads through Google AdMob, configured for non-personalized ads (npa=1) with a child-directed and maximum “G” content rating setting. Non-personalized ads use coarse, non-identifying signals to serve a relevant-but-not-profiled ad. We do not request App Tracking Transparency permission and do not access the IDFA for tracking. Google’s handling of ad requests is governed by Google’s Privacy Policy.

3. In-app purchases (Apple). The optional “Remove Ads” purchase is processed entirely by Apple through the App Store. We do not see or store your payment information. Apple provides us only an anonymous receipt confirming the purchase, which we use to unlock the ad-free entitlement on your device.

4. Leaderboard (Apple Game Center). If you use the optional “Total Laziness” leaderboard, your score and Game Center nickname are handled by Apple Game Center under Apple’s Privacy Policy. This is optional and off unless you open the leaderboard.

5. Analytics. The App currently includes no third-party analytics or crash-reporting SDK and sends us no usage telemetry.

Children

Lazy Dad is a family-friendly comedy suitable for all ages. We treat all users as potentially children: ads are configured child-directed and non-personalized, and we collect no personal information from anyone. We comply with the U.S. Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and the App Store’s Kids/Family requirements.

Your rights (GDPR / CCPA)

Because we do not collect personal data and do not track you, there is no personal profile to access, correct, or delete on our side. Ad and purchase data are governed by Google’s and Apple’s respective policies, where you can exercise your rights. You can delete all local game data at any time by deleting the App.

Data retention

We retain no personal data on our servers (we operate none for this App). Local game data persists on your device until you delete the App.

Changes

We may update this policy; the “Last updated” date will change. Material changes will be reflected in an App update.
